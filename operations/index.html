Scholarships

A limited number of scholarships are available for each course. Generally, we limit the number of scholarships to 2-4 depending on the size of the class. Classes are incredibly popular and fill up quickly, so we have the money to pay for more scholarships, but we don't have enough teachers and TAs to up the overall capacity.

General Info

  • Scholarship application deadline is 2 weeks before the class
  • Scholarship applicants should be notified of their acceptance or rejection one week before the class
  • Approved applicants should reply within a timeframe indicating their acceptance
  • There is a very small number of banned applicants, see the applications document below
  • Applicants who are no shows are ineligible for all future scholarships with GDI

Scholarship Resources

  • Scholarship application form and responses
  • Email template - approved scholarship recipient
  • Email template - rejected scholarship recipient

The deadline to apply for a scholarship is [2 WEEKS BEFORE CLASS]. Scholarship applicants will be notified shortly after the deadline. Class capacity does not impact scholarship students.

Procedure

  1. Once a month, send an email using TAMS to all TAs so they can sign up for classes.
  2. Create a rough outline of the course schedule for the upcoming quarter.
  3. Check the course materials to ensure that they're in teachable condition.
  4. Contact your preferred teachers for each class and confirm the dates with them.
  5. Reach out to preferred locations for classroom space.
    • Offer the company hosting one spot for an employee to attend for free to supervise the course (not included in course capacity count).
    • Make sure there are logo and bio materials for the Meetup group.
    • Note whether or not the location requires students to present their ID to security before being let up.
    • For all day workshops: ask that the location provide breakfast for the students, teacher, and TAs.
  6. Schedule the Meetup and open RSVPs immediately.
  7. Designate a GDI staff member to attend the event as a representative or organizer. This is typically a co-leader or assistant organizer.
  8. For classes that require machine setup, provide concise instructions in the course description and link to a comprehensive install guide. Additionally, message students a week before class reminding them to complete the installation.
  9. One month before class
    • Email the materials and course website to the instructor.
    • Setup the instructor with HQ for payments.
      • Organizer: send Payee setup template to instructor
      • Teacher: New Payee Form
      • Teacher: email copy of W9 to payments@girldevelopit.com
  10. Two weeks before class
    • Due date for scholarship applications
  11. One week before class
    • Send email confirmation to all TAs
    • Send information email and outline of the day's/evening's events to the location hosting the class. Confirm your arrival and departure times, and what level of setup is required
    • Confirm that your GDI representative is able to attend
    • Notify scholarship applicants
  12. A day or two before class
    • Email confirmation to students with info about arrival times and things they need to bring
    • Send list of student names to hosting location (if needed for security)
    • If there are materials to download email a link to the class website
  13. The day of class, the attending GDI representative should:
    • Arrive 15-30 minutes early to setup
    • Take student attendance
  14. The day after class
    • The GDI representative who attended emails the names of any absent students to the organizer of the class
    • Organizer submits class payment request
      • Payment request form
    • Organizer sends feedback survey to students
      • Student course survey
